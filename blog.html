Addressing Bias in Venture Capital

Insights from Reach Capital's Journey

In an era where systemic inequities are more glaring than ever, the venture capital industry is not immune to the critical examinations of its practices, especially in terms of diversity and inclusion. One compelling case study in this area is that of Reach Capital, a venture firm that has taken significant strides to mitigate bias and promote diversity within its investment portfolio.

The Roots of Bias in Venture Capital

Historically, venture capital has been dominated by networks that often exclude underrepresented groups, including women and people of color. This lack of diversity can lead to a cycle where only certain types of founders receive funding, perpetuating existing disparities. For instance, in 2020, less than one percent of all venture funding went to Black founders, highlighting a persistent and pervasive issue across the industry.

Reach Capital's Proactive Approach

Founded in 2015, Reach Capital started with a mission to increase access to opportunity through education. The founders, Jennifer Carolan and Shauntel Garvey, emphasized creating a diverse investment team and portfolio to reflect the communities they serve. By 2020, Reach had managed to double the percentage of female-founded companies in their portfolio to 40 percent and significantly increase the representation of Latinx-founded companies.

Uncovering and Addressing Unconscious Bias

Reach's introspection revealed several unconscious biases that affected their decision-making. To combat this, Reach expanded their networks and support systems, engaging with accelerators and funds that focus on underrepresented founders. They also adjusted their evaluation criteria to ensure they weren't holding diverse founders to a higher standard or missing out on high-potential opportunities due to biased perceptions.

Continuous Improvement and Industry Influence

Recognizing that addressing bias is an ongoing process, Reach Capital continues to refine their strategies. They now regularly gather and reflect on diversity data, use it to inform their investment decisions, and share their findings publicly to foster broader industry change.

Conclusion

Reach Capital's efforts provide a blueprint for other venture capital firms to follow.
